November

The guards are rough with you. Or maybe it's just because you're blindfolded. Either way, they blindfolded everyone and took you outside, in front of the chanting mob that just took over the embassy. Now you're back inside, blindfold off, and the guards are still rough - both with you, and their duties. There is a chance to escape while they're dealing with someone else, if you're quiet and careful.

Do you attempt to escape while the guards are distracted?

Yes No

December

There are more guards now, and it's a good thing you didn't try escaping. Bazargan's cabinet resigned, and Khomeini wasted no time in sanctioning the takeover of the embassy. Despite releasing some women and black folks, their numbers were swiftly replaced by escapees and some people who weren't even here during the takeover. The new guards are certainly not friendly, barking orders not to speak, but whispers that the former Shah left the US and the US suing Iran to the International Court of Justice still make their way to you.

Do you continue passing on the rumours?

Whisper about the Shah leaving the US Whisper about US suing Iran

January

The guards are angry now, but tighter with their security. Apparently, Canada helped some diplomats escape Iran, and now everyone is tied at nearly all times. Crowds outside keep protesting, against seemingly everything the US does in regards to Iran. They declare you are guests and the US has no reason to be so antagonistic.

A guard swaggers over, saying that if you don't want to be a guest, you can tell him now.

Tell him you don't want to be a guest Stay silent

February

Someone else said they didn't want to be a guest, and you saw the cruel light in the guard's eyes. The person was picked up and carried somewhere away from everyone else, deeper into the embassy. Several guards hold them as they are blindfolded. They attempt to stop them and are struck harshly.

Do you do anything to help them?

Turn away from the scene Yes, I attempt to stand despite my bindings

March

Guards are talking nastily about the Shah. It seems he has left America and has gone to Egypt for treatment now. They are angry that America didn't force him out and are looking for a way to vent their anger - as usual, they turn to you, their "guests". They want to include you in a game of Russian Roulette.

You don't have a choice, but how do you react?

In visible fear Stay as calm as possible

April

America has cut ties with Iran now, and placed embargos. The guards still nastily wonder why America is so mad - their "guests" are alive and well right behind them. Despite being a "guest", you aren't allowed to do anything. Once, though, you catch a sliver of hope in a whispered conversation. America attempted another rescue mission that led to failure. Unfortunately, the guard notices you and asks:

Do you believe in rescue?

Rescue from what? (Lie)No

May

It seems the rescue attempt by America was real, and so everyone is being split up and scattered around to prevent a successful second attempt. The public isn't allowed to know how their "guests" are actually being treated, so you all had to be untied. With the chaos of moving everyone while also terrorizing them, there is a decent chance to escape and rescue yourself.

Take a chance?

Yes No

June

In your new prison, the doors are once again chained, but at least it's harder for the guards to separate you out. That said, one of your fellow prisoners hasn't been doing too well recently. He seems to be seriously ill, but it doesn't stop the guards from beating him. However, they might let him out early if you can convince them that he is too sick to make it through this.

Do you attempt to convince the guards?

Yes No

July

The sick man was released, and you hope he is able to be treated for whatever it was. More importantly, it seems the Shah died recently, and the guards are rejoicing. The games and threats have stopped at least for today, maybe even forever. Maybe today, while the Shah is still newly dead, you could convince them they no longer have a reason to keep you as their "guest".

Try to negotiate with them?

Negotiate Don't dream

August

All the hostages are being moved again, but this time, no-one knows why. Again, it seems like you could escape, but you aren't the only one who noticed. Another hostage invites you to attempt to escape with them. If one of you can't escape, it could at least distract the guards long enough for the other to run away. If one of you gets out, you could get help.

Will you join the escape plan?

Yes - at least one of us will make it No - I don't want to risk recapture

September

The guards are pleased again. Ruhollah has put new conditions on your release - all of the Shah's assets have to be released from his American accounts. They are happy that they can make demands of America, and that in their eyes, America will dance like a puppet on strings. It makes you angry, but then again, if America gives in, you could be going home soon.

What do you do?

America will never give in! America will never give in.

November

Another move, and this time you don't entertain the idea of escaping. You've already seen twice what happens to those who get caught. When you get to your new location for however long this will last, the doors are still chained - but there are full bathrooms, with running hot water. You want to rejoice, but the guards are still outside the door.

Do you exclaim or quietly enjoy this luxury?

Be quiet and use water sparingly Rejoice and take a bath, finally

December

No letters have arrived for anyone, but several people claim their family would write to them and the guards must be keeping the mail. You know the guards are absolutely keeping the mail, but the guards are cruel and do it purely so they can taunt anyone who wants their mail. Some people can't handle the constant silence from home and are begging for even just confirmation someone sent a letter.

Do you call them out?

Yes - I want my mail too No - I have had enough torment

January

You can't believe it. After everything that has happened and more than a year as a hostage, you are finally being released. You made it through this ordeal relatively unscathed, which is signifantly better than what some others have gone through. Everyone made it out alive, and all it took was a new president for Iran to finally release you.

HOORAY!
