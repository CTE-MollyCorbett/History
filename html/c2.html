December

There are more guards now, and you have been separated from everyone else for attempting to escape. Your cell is cold and bare, with only the guards talking outside the door and occasionally taunting you. Bazargan's cabinet resigned, and Khomeini wasted no time in sanctioning the takeover of the embassy. Despite releasing some women and black folks, their numbers were swiftly replaced by escapees and some people who weren't even here during the takeover. The new guards are not friendly, having ordered you not to speak, but still tell of how the former Shah left the US and scoff at how the US is suing Iran to the International Court of Justice.

Do you say anything to the guards?

Whisper about the Shah leaving the US Ask about the US suing Iran

January

You are back with the group, beaten for "dissenting" while in confinement, and the guards are angry now. They have tightened their security on the outside. Apparently, Canada helped some diplomats escape Iran, and now everyone is tied at nearly all times. Crowds outside keep protesting, against seemingly everything the US does in regards to Iran. They declare you are guests and the US has no reason to be so antagonistic, despite you having already seen otherwise.

A guard swaggers over, saying that if you don't want to be a guest, you can tell him now.

Tell him you don't want to be a guest Stay silent

February

As you said didn't want to be a guest, you saw the cruel light in the guard's eyes. You were picked up and carried somewhere away from everyone else, deeper into the embassy. Several guards hold you as you are blindfolded. As you struggle against them, you are struck harshly.

Do you continue to fight them?

No, I already know what is coming Yes, I attempt to fight despite my bindings

March

In solitary confinement, the guards are talking nastily about the Shah. You sit in the mock electric chair and listen to what they say. It seems he has left America and has gone to Egypt for treatment now. They are angry that America didn't force the Shah out and are looking for a way to vent their anger - as usual, they turn to you, their "guest". They want to include you in a game of Russian Roulette.

You don't have a choice, but how do you react?

In visible fear Stay as calm as possible

April

America has cut ties with Iran now, and placed embargos. The guards still nastily wonder why America is so mad - their "guests" are alive and well right behind them. Despite being a "guest", you aren't allowed to do anything. They, however, are certainly allowed to shoot you - which is why you are being guarded in a makeshift infirmary instead of with the group. Once, though, you catch a sliver of hope in a whispered conversation. America attempted another rescue mission that led to failure. Unfortunately, the guard notices you listening and asks:

Do you believe in rescue?

Rescue from what? (Lie)No

May

After more threats of execution as a demonstration for what you should be rescued from, it seems the rescue attempt by America was real, and so everyone is being split up and scattered around to prevent a successful second attempt. The public isn't allowed to know how their "guests" are actually being treated, so you all had to be untied. With the chaos of moving everyone while also terrorizing them, there is a decent chance to escape and rescue yourself.

Take a chance?

Yes No

June

In your fancy new cell, the doors are once again chained, but at least it's harder for the guards to separate you out and punish you. That said, one of your fellow prisoners hasn't been doing too well recently. He seems to be seriously ill, but it doesn't stop the guards from beating him. However, they might let him out early if you can convince them that he is too sick to make it through this.

Do you attempt to convince the guards?

Yes No

July

The sick man was released, though only after he became immobile, and you hope he is able to be treated for whatever it was. More importantly, it seems the Shah died recently, and the guards are rejoicing. The games and threats have stopped at least for today, maybe even forever. Maybe today, while the Shah is still newly dead, you could convince them they no longer have a reason to keep you as their "guest".

Try to negotiate with them?

Negotiate Don't dream

August

All the hostages are being moved again, but this time, no-one knows why. This time, you are being moved while still tied up and bruised. Again, it seems like you could escape, but you aren't the only one who noticed. Another hostage invites you to attempt to escape with them. If one of you can't escape, it could at least distract the guards long enough for the other to run away. If one of you gets out, you could get help.

Will you join the escape plan?

Yes - at least one of us will make it No - I don't want to risk recapture

September

Despite recent attempts at death by multiple captives, as well as your own attempt at escape, the guards are pleased again. Ruhollah has put new conditions on your release - all of the Shah's assets have to be released from his American accounts. They are happy that they can make demands of America, and that in their eyes, America will dance like a puppet on strings. It makes you angry, but then again, if America gives in, you could be going home soon - and you could leave your solitary, freezing cell.

What do you do?

America will never give in! America will never give in.

November

Another move, and this time you don't entertain the idea of escaping. You've already experienced twice what happens to those who get caught. When you get to your new location for however long this will last, the doors are still chained - but there are full bathrooms, with running hot water. You want to rejoice, but the guards are still outside the door and you are no longer sure of the limits of human cruelty.

Do you exclaim or quietly enjoy this luxury?

Be quiet and use water sparingly Rejoice and take a bath, finally

December

The facilities have improved, but as you have found out, the guards' moods have not, and fresh bruises and cuts bear testimony to that. No letters have arrived for anyone, but several people claim their family would write to them and the guards must be keeping the mail. You know the guards are absolutely keeping the mail, but the guards are cruel and do it purely so they can taunt anyone who wants their mail. Some people can't handle the constant silence from home and are begging for even just confirmation someone sent a letter. The guards are particularly cruel to one man who wants to see the letters from his wife, saying she must have moved on if he isn't recieving anything.

Do you call them out?

Yes - I want my mail too No - I have had enough torment

January

You can't believe it. After everything that has happened and more than a year as a hostage, you are finally being released. You made it through this ordeal alive, which is at least something, no matter how dead you are inside. All this fighting, and terror, and abuse, and all it took was a new president for Iran to finally release you. Why couldn't Carter have simply stepped down sooner?

Hooray?
